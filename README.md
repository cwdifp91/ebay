## Money Back Guarantee: How Protected Are You Really?

eBay promotes their Money Back Guarantee pretty heavily, so let's look at what it actually covers.

**What's Covered:**
- Item doesn't arrive
- Item arrives damaged
- Item doesn't match the listing description
- Item is faulty (even if not disclosed)

**Coverage includes:**
- Full purchase price
- Original shipping costs
- Applies to virtually all items on eBay.com

**The Process:**
1. Contact the seller first through the Resolution Center
2. Give them at least 3 business days to respond
3. Escalate to eBay if needed (within 30 days of delivery)
4. eBay investigates and issues refund if appropriate

**What's NOT Covered:**
- Motor vehicles (separate Vehicle Purchase Protection exists)
- Real estate
- Classified listings
- Situations where you just changed your mind (unless seller offers returns)

The Money Back Guarantee is automaticâ€”no extra fees, no signing up. It's just part of buying on eBay.

For high-value items like watches ($2,000+) and luxury goods, eBay also offers Authenticity Guarantee, where third-party experts verify items before they ship to you. This adds another layer of protection against counterfeits.

## What About Customer Service?

This is where things get complicated. Based on recent customer feedback, eBay's customer service is... inconsistent.

**The Good:**
- Automated systems handle straightforward issues quickly
- Money Back Guarantee process is relatively smooth for clear-cut cases
- Resolution Center provides a structured dispute process

**The Challenges:**
- Phone support is difficult to reach
- Case decisions can feel automated and inflexible
- Sellers report feeling buyers are favored in disputes
- Buyers report difficulty when sellers don't cooperate

**Practical Advice:**
- Document everything: screenshots, messages, tracking info
- Communicate through eBay's messaging system (creates a record)
- Be specific when opening cases
- Act within the 30-day window

The system works better when you have clear evidence and follow the prescribed process. It gets messy in gray areas.
